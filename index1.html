Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Use the interactive charts below to explore the dataset. The dashboard displays a variety of charts that provide visualizations of the data, including a bar chart of the top ten bacterial species found, a bubble chart that shows the frequency of each bacterial species, and a gauge chart that shows the frequency of belly button washing by participants. Users can select different test subject IDs to view the bacterial data and demographic information associated with each subject. The dashboard is designed to be user-friendly and provides an engaging and interactive way to explore the dataset.

The bar chart displays the top 10 bacterial species (OTUs) found in the selected individual's belly button. The x-axis shows the OTU IDs and the y-axis shows the sample values, which are a measure of the amount of bacteria for each OTU.

The gauge chart shows the frequency of belly button washing by the selected individual. The washing frequency is measured in scrubs per week and is displayed on a scale from 0 to 9.

The bubble chart shows the frequency of each bacterial species (OTU) found in the selected individual's belly button. The size of each bubble corresponds to the sample value, and the color represents the OTU ID.
